The Class of Sindhis

The Sindhi community is renowned for its profound connection to its ancient culture and mystical traditions. Their souls are deeply moved by soulful Sufi music and folk poetry, which resonate at the heart of their identity. This deep love for art and heritage, vibrantly displayed in their celebrations, fills their lives with spiritual warmth and a strong sense of pride.

The City of Quaid

The "City of Quaid" is a title lovingly given to Karachi, Pakistan's largest metropolis. It earned this name as it is both the birthplace and the final resting place of the nation's founder, Quaid-e-Azam Muhammad Ali Jinnah. His magnificent white marble mausoleum, Mazar-e-Quaid, stands as the city's most iconic symbol.
