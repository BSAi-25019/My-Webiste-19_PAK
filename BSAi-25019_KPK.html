The KHANS & their History

In Khyber Pakhtunkhwa (KPK), "Khan" is a title of great respect, historically used for Pashtun tribal chiefs, leaders, and influential landowners. Originating from a Turco-Mongolic word for "ruler," the title was adopted by Pashtun society to denote nobility and leadership. While it once signified a person's high status, "Khan" is now also one of the most common surnames in the region, carried by descendants of these prominent families.

Khyber Pass

The Khyber Pass is a legendary mountain pass in Khyber Pakhtunkhwa, Pakistan, connecting the city of Peshawar to Afghanistan. For millennia, it served as a vital part of the ancient Silk Road and, most famously, as the main gateway for countless invasions into the Indian subcontinent.

The Skill on Architecture

The architecture of KPK is a blend of ancient, regional, and colonial styles, influenced by its geography and history. It includes the ancient Gandhara style with Greco-Buddhist elements, Islamic and Mughal designs like the Bala Hisar Fort and Mohabbat Khan Mosque, and colonial structures reflecting the British influence, such as buildings in Peshawar.
