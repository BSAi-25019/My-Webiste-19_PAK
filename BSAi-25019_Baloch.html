Balochi Culture

Balochi culture is deeply rooted in strong tribal traditions, a legendary code of honor, and immense hospitality. It is visually famous for its distinctive clothing: men wear wide, flowing shalwar kameez and turbans, while women wear vibrant, loose-fitting dresses adorned with intricate, colorful Balochi embroidery ('Doch').

Land & Minerals

Pakistan's largest province by area, a vast and rugged land defined by arid mountains, extensive deserts, and a long coastline on the Arabian Sea. This landscape holds immense mineral wealth, including one of the world's largest deposits of copper and gold (at Reko Diq) and Pakistan's most significant reserves of natural gas, coal, and chromite.

History of Ziarat

Ziarat is a famous hill station near Quetta, renowned for housing the Quaid-e-Azam Residency, the historic wooden building where Muhammad Ali Jinnah spent his final days. The town is also celebrated for its ancient, slow-growing juniper forests, which are among the oldest and largest in the world.
