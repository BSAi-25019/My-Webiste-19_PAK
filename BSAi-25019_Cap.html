The Capital

Islamabad is the purpose-built capital of Pakistan, a modern, planned city established in the 1960s and celebrated for its organized sectors and lush greenery. It is nestled against the backdrop of the scenic Margalla Hills

The Modern Pakistan

As the face of modern Pakistan, Islamabad is a meticulously planned capital, standing in contrast to the nation's ancient, organic cities. It functions as the clean, green, and orderly administrative heart of the country, reflecting a forward-looking vision and modern infrastructure.

The Clenaest & Worth Seeing

Islamabad is widely regarded as Pakistan's cleanest and greenest city, thanks to its modern planning, well-maintained sectors, and the lush Margalla Hills National Park that forms its northern backdrop. The most worth-seeing sights are the iconic, tent-shaped Faisal Mosque and the Pakistan Monument on the Shakarparian Hills, which offers panoramic views of the entire capital.
